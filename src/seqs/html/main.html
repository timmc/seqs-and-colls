Collections and Sequences in Clojure

Introduction

The topic of collections and sequences in Clojure can be quite confusing for beginners. In this document I aim to explain how these two abstractions differ and how they work together.

Collections

A collection may be identified by the predicate coll?. Collections may be associative, sequential, both, or neither. Associative colls support key-value lookups. Maps are the traditional associative data structure, but vectors can be treated as mappings of indices to values. Sequential colls retain a linear ordering under insertion and deletion. Lists and vectors have this property. Note that a vector is both sequential and associative while a set is neither.

Sequences

A sequence is a data structure that is expected to be accessed in a sequential manner. It may be infinitely long, and may require additional computation in order to read.

Relationship between the two

  • All sequences are also collections.
  • You can derive a sequence backed by collection.
  • You can read any sequence into a new collection.

Nota Bene: Sequences are implemented as lists.
