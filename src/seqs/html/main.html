The first abstraction is the collection, defined by the predicate coll?. Collections may be associative, sequential, both, or neither. Associative colls support key-value lookups. Maps are the traditional associative data structure, but vectors can be treated as mappings of indices to values. Sequential colls retain a linear ordering under insertion and deletion. Lists and vectors have this property. Note that a vector is both sequential and associative while a set is neither.
